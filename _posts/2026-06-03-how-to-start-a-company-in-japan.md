Starting a company in Japan is not simple. Somehow it's both traditional and modernized. This post is a summary of my trip doing it in Japan. Hopefull you find it useful.

You can of course outsourcing this to a lawyer. But you know that can be quite expensive. 

## Eligibility

The first thing you need to do is to check your VISA type. Most foreigners come to Japan because of work. In these cases, foreigners are on the so called working visa. If you check your Residence Card(在留卡), you should see this: 技術・人文知識・国際業務. The company hires you is the sponsor of the visa. So while you can register a company, you cannot work full-time for your own company. You may ask, can I work as a second job, for example, only after working for the sponser. The answer is, it depends. Some companies actually disallow you to have a second job(副业). So you also need to chekc the regulations of your own companies.

Some of the people are holding high-skilled visa(高度専門職). If are holding 高度専門職 II visa, you are allowed to register the company and work for it. Because this is still a  visa with sponsorship, you need to check whether your sponser allow you to do so. 

If you are a PR(永住者), then you have no restrictions legally but again, you need to check whether your employer allows to you have a second job.

## Things must have

Once you know you can register a company in Japan, next is to ensure you have the following items. That will make your application so much easier.

### A Japanese phone number

Needless to say, there are countless places that a valid phone number is required.

### A personal Japanese bank account

This is required when you need to show you have the capital to start the company, while the amount could be as low as 1 JPY, you need to prove you have 1 JPY in a bank account.

### Personal stamp certificate(**印鑑証明書**)

For people who have lived in Japan for quite sometime, you should know what it is. If you don't have one, you can get it from your local city hall(区役所).

## Things better to have

### My ID number

While this is optional, but I cannot imagine how difficult the process would be if I did not have one. During the process, you need to prove the operator is actually "you" for many times.

If you have all the documents mentioned above, congratulations! The rest is just following processes.

## Step 0: Company name, company type, company address

> There are only two hard things in Computer Science: cache invalidation and naming things

And Japan certainly makes it even harder, because you actually name your company in the following way:

株式会社xyz or xyz株式会社. They are both valid.  Interestingly, according to my research, the former is more common wihtin Japan. You can also mix Kanji with English letters, numbers.

Once you know the name, then you need to decide the type: KK(株式会社) or GK(合同会社).

I'm not going to cover the detail difference here. But if you choose KK, you need to publish your company details. That means, you need a company address.

If you are going to rent an office, then of course it's simple. But if you are not planning as I was, you need to find a address that can be the company address.

Depending on your residence type, if you live in a shared building such as マンション or アパート, it's most likely you are disallowed to use the address as a corporate address. If you live in a 一户建, you can certainly use it. However, that's a risk because you are exposing your own private address.

This is when virtual office(バーチャルオフィス) comes to rescue. Simply put, they rent a physical office, then they rent the address to several companies, mails sent to these address will be forwarded manually to company representative later. One thing to be noted, if you are choosing virtual office, when you select the bank for the company, you also need to select the ones that support virtual office. We will cover it later.

I'm using [DMM](https://virtualoffice.dmm.com/) for the service.

## Step 1: Full DIY or use a SaaS

Once you know what kind of companies you are going to open, the next step is to decide whether to go to full DIY or using a SaaS to help you. 

When I faced such situation, my thinking was quite simple. I'm going to open a KK, I'm not a legal expert. Going full DIY is too much of a work. As a result, I went to [freee会社設立](https://www.freee.co.jp/launch/). 

## Step2: Creating 定款 

In the English word, 定款 is called Articles of Incorporation. It contains all the details of the company. One of the most important part of 定款 is to decide the business category your company will do. While you are free to add many, it's recommended to include only the core ones that are related to your business. And it's also highly recommended to put

> 前各号に附帯又は関連する一切の業務

as the last. 

If you need to change the business category, you need to go to the 法务局. But it's not a complex process to do.

If you are using Freee, they will recommend a lawyer to you. Once you input the name, address, business categories such details in Freee, the lawer will generate the 定款 for you.

In order for the lawyers to create the 定款, you need the following documents:

- 印鑑証明書
- ID (passport, my ID card, etc)

## Step 3: Capital verification 

You need to prove that you have the enough capital that you claim in the 定款. Of course, this will be based on your personal account now. You need to show:

- You need to show the amount of money specefied in the 定款 has been wired in on or after the date the 定款 was created.

You can simply make the screenshots of your bank account app and upload to Freee. It has detail docs on teaching how to make the screenshots. 

## Step 4: Submit your application & 定款認証

This is a step required you to speak basic Japanese or having somebody help you because an interview is required. But don't worry, everything can be done online.

However, if you want to do online, from my understanding, you need to have your my number card because this is formal legal process that require digital authentication using your my number card with a phone or a card reader that can do the digital signature.

While it can be done online, it is still one of the most gruelling processes. Essentially, you need to:

1. Submit the application to the Legal Affairs Bureau 24 hours before your 定款認証 interview. 
2. Finish the interview with 公証役場 and wait for the result.

If you are using Freee, it will guide you through the process including submitting the application to the bureau. After submitting the application, your application will be carefully reviewed by the official. If there is anything missing, your application will be withdrawn and a message will be sent to you for fixing. From my experience, I needed to re-submit some of the materials before they finally approved the application and arranged the interview. Afer that,  the interview is only a matter of formality.

Once this step is finished, you can take a deep breath: **Because it's almost done**. You will receive the certificate from your Legal Affairs Bureau and most importantly, your 法人番号.

## Step 5: Getting your companies legal documents

While this can be done electronically, but a bug at the Legal Affairs Bureau prevent me doing so. I still went to the physical office and picked up:

- 登記事項証明書
- 印鑑証明書

Above documents are required when you apply the bank account for the company.

Since you are already at the office, it's better to also apply the 印鑑カード at the same time. 

Again, Freee will provide necessary guide for this process. The date on the certificate is also the official date of the company establishment.

## Step 6: Notify 年金事務所 and tax offices

If you are going to pay anybody in the company (including your self), you need to notify 年金事務所. You need to make such decisions within 3 months since the company establishment.

Within 2 months, you need to make electronic applications to your local tax offices. While doing so, you should know that 青色申告の承認申請 is a helpful option. Because it can help to reduce taxes in the future. 

## Step 7: Opening a bank account

We've gone really far!! This is the last step. Hopefully you are still there. :P

But unfortunately, as you probably can expect, opening a bank account is again, not an easy step.

For small companies owned by foreigners , from what I heard, having an account in the big banks such as MUFG is quite difficult. So I directly go for the smaller options, such as GMO or Rakuten bank. After consulting with AI, I choose GMO. 

Again, you need to fillup a big form, sending necessary documents, prove yourself identity and wait for the approval.

And I found this is the most difficult. 

For my service, I'm using [Paddle](https://paddle.com/) as the payment service. The reviewers at the banks really could not understand how the money flows around between my service, Paddle and the banks. Since Paddle does not offer receipts in Japanese, it makes things more difficult. So my applications were being rejected multiple times due to reviewers could not identify my real intent. I called to the customer support, they did not have a clue but provided some hints, I made some adjustments, got rejected again, back and forth. After almost a month, I realised that it's better to write a complete guideline: from user signing up, to selecting the plan, to pay the service and finally show the balance at Paddle side all with translation. I made the submission and finally I got the approval!

So some rules of thumb:

- If you are using virtual service, make sure they send the necessary docs via 転送不要の簡易書留. So your virtual service can forward the mail to you without presenting an ID card.
- Also make sure the banks support the companies using virtual service. 
- Make sure the banks can understand how the money flows through your systems so they know why you need a bank. That will make the review process so much smoother.
